## Idea
Every government circular that changes a rule is supposed to update four separate systems at once — the online portal, the officer's SOP, the citizen application form, and the notification sent to the public. In reality, nobody owns that handoff, so systems fall out of sync for months. For example, when a circular shortens an income certificate's validity from 12 months to 6, the portal often keeps accepting 12-month-old certificates unnoticed — wrongly approving or rejecting real applicants until an audit surfaces it. This isn't hypothetical: the National Scholarship Portal (140+ schemes, ₹2,731 Cr disbursed in AY2020-21 alone) failed to verify over 1,000 minority-community girls' applications in Gujarat in 2022, and they missed their scholarships entirely.
NiyamGuard AI closes this gap. Fed a circular, it extracts the exact rule that changed — old value, new value, effective date — and checks every dependent system against it, flagging mismatches with a visual redline diff and a confidence score, so ambiguous cases are surfaced rather than guessed at. It traces downstream impact (an out-of-sync portal means the officer and the citizen are out of sync too) and produces a prioritized, harm-ranked fix list with every flag traceable back to its source clause. It drafts citizen notifications for human approval and logs every action in an immutable audit trail — but it never enforces policy itself; a human approves every real-world update.
NiyamGuard doesn't summarize circulars or answer questions about them — it continuously verifies that reality matches policy, closing a structural coordination gap that today depends entirely on someone remembering to check.
